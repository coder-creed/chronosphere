Time, Explained

Tap a concept to expand.

UTC — Coordinated Universal Time

UTC is the world's primary time standard. It descends from GMT (Greenwich Mean Time) but is governed by an ensemble of 400+ atomic clocks across 80+ labs.

Every timezone is an offset from UTC: New York is UTC−5 in winter, UTC−4 in summer. UTC itself never observes DST.

GMT vs UTC

GMT is a timezone (the legal time of the UK in winter). UTC is a time standard. In casual use they're identical, but UTC is precise to the nanosecond, while GMT was originally astronomical.

They can drift apart by up to 0.9 s — corrected by leap seconds.

Daylight Saving Time

DST shifts clocks forward in spring and back in autumn. Roughly 40% of countries do it. Equatorial nations rarely bother — day length barely changes.

Arizona (except the Navajo Nation), Hawaii, and most of Asia skip it entirely.

Time Zones — why 24 isn't 24

Earth has 38+ time zones. Some use 30- or 45-minute offsets: India is UTC+5:30, Nepal UTC+5:45.

China spans 5 geographical zones but uses just one (UTC+8). Russia uses 11.

Leap Seconds

Earth's rotation is slowing. The IERS inserts a leap second when needed — making 23:59:60 a valid moment.

27 leap seconds since 1972. The CGPM voted to abolish them by 2035 — they wreak havoc on finance and GPS.

Unix Time

Seconds elapsed since 1970-01-01 00:00:00 UTC. Currently about 1.7 billion.

32-bit signed integers overflow at 2038-01-19 03:14:07 UTC — the Y2038 problem. 64-bit is good until the heat death of the sun.

Atomic Clocks

The SI second = 9,192,631,770 oscillations of caesium-133. Optical lattice clocks (strontium, ytterbium) are 100× more accurate — drift one second every 15 billion years.

GPS requires nanosecond sync, since light travels ~30 cm per nanosecond.

Time Dilation

Einstein: time flows slower in stronger gravity and at higher speeds. GPS satellites' clocks tick +45 µs/day from weaker gravity and −7 µs/day from orbital speed. Net: +38µs/day.

Without correcting for this, GPS positions would drift by ~10 km/day.

ISO 8601

The unambiguous format: 2026-05-31T14:30:00Z. Z = UTC ("Zulu"). Always sortable as a string.

Chess Clock Modes

Sudden Death: fixed time, whoever flags loses.

Fischer: a fixed increment is added after each move.

Bronstein: clock pauses N seconds before draining. You never gain time, only avoid losing it on quick moves.

How the globe knows where pins go

Each city has lat/lon. To map to a 3D sphere:

φ = (90 − lat) × π/180
θ = (lon + 180) × π/180
x = −r·sin(φ)·cos(θ)
y = r·cos(φ)
z = r·sin(φ)·sin(θ)

IANA timezone strings (e.g. America/Los_Angeles) are resolved via the browser's Intl.DateTimeFormat, which handles DST automatically.
