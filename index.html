Time, Explained

Tap a concept to expand.

UTC — Coordinated Universal Time

UTC is the world's primary time standard. It descends from GMT but is governed by an ensemble of 400+ atomic clocks across 80+ labs.

Every timezone is an offset from UTC: New York is UTC−5 in winter, UTC−4 in summer. UTC itself never observes DST.

The day/night terminator

The terminator is the line dividing day from night on Earth. It moves westward at roughly 1670 km/h at the equator.

It isn't perfectly sharp — atmospheric scattering creates twilight zones spanning ~18° of arc. The Sun's declination (currently ±23.45°) tilts the terminator north or south through the seasons.

Switch to Live mode to see the actual terminator on the globe right now.

GMT vs UTC

GMT is a timezone (the legal time of the UK in winter). UTC is a time standard. In casual use they're identical, but UTC is precise to the nanosecond, while GMT was originally astronomical.

They can drift apart by up to 0.9 s — corrected by leap seconds.

Daylight Saving Time

DST shifts clocks forward in spring and back in autumn. Roughly 40% of countries do it. Equatorial nations rarely bother.

Arizona, Hawaii, and most of Asia skip it entirely.

Time Zones — why 24 isn't 24

Earth has 38+ time zones. Some use 30- or 45-minute offsets: India is UTC+5:30, Nepal UTC+5:45.

China spans 5 geographical zones but uses just one (UTC+8). Russia uses 11.

Leap Seconds

Earth's rotation is slowing. The IERS inserts a leap second when needed — making 23:59:60 a valid moment.

27 leap seconds since 1972. The CGPM voted to abolish them by 2035.

Unix Time

Seconds since 1970-01-01 00:00:00 UTC. Currently about 1.7 billion.

32-bit signed integers overflow at 2038-01-19 03:14:07 UTC — the Y2038 problem.

Atomic Clocks

The SI second = 9,192,631,770 oscillations of caesium-133. Optical lattice clocks are 100× more accurate — drift one second every 15 billion years.

Time Dilation

Einstein: time flows slower in stronger gravity and at higher speeds. GPS satellite clocks tick +38 µs/day faster than ground clocks. Without correction, GPS positions would drift ~10 km/day.

Chess Clock Modes

Sudden Death: fixed time, whoever flags loses.

Fischer: a fixed increment is added after each move.

Bronstein: clock pauses N seconds before draining. You never gain time, only avoid losing it on quick moves.

How the globe knows where pins go

Each city has lat/lon. To map to a 3D sphere:

φ = (90 − lat) × π/180
θ = (lon + 180) × π/180
x = −r·sin(φ)·cos(θ)
y = r·cos(φ)
z = r·sin(φ)·sin(θ)

IANA timezone strings are resolved via the browser's Intl.DateTimeFormat, which handles DST automatically.
